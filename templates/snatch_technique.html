The Snatch - Olympic Weightlifting Guide

Understanding The Snatch

The snatch is a dynamic and complex Olympic lift that requires the athlete to lift the barbell from the ground to an overhead position in one continuous motion.

Focus: This lift tests the lifterâ€™s strength, coordination, balance, and flexibility, all while demanding explosive power and speed.

The Setup

Start with a wide grip on the barbell, feet shoulder-width apart, and the bar positioned over the mid-foot. Your hips should be lower than your shoulders, with your chest up and back straight.

First Pull

Lift the barbell off the ground by extending your knees and hips simultaneously. Keep the bar close to your body and maintain a neutral spine throughout the pull.

Second Pull

Once the bar reaches knee height, explosively extend your hips and knees while pulling the bar upward with your arms. The goal is to generate as much upward force as possible.

The Catch

As the bar reaches its highest point, drop under it by pulling yourself into a squat position. Catch the bar overhead with your arms fully extended, ensuring stability.

Important: Ensure the bar is directly overhead and in line with your body to avoid imbalances.

Recovery

Once the bar is secure overhead in a squat position, stand up by driving through your heels and extending your legs. Ensure the bar stays overhead throughout the motion until fully standing.

Monitoring & Adjusting Your Training

1. Heart Rate Monitoring

Tracking your heart rate during cardiovascular training is essential to ensure you're working at the right intensity. Use a fitness tracker or heart rate monitor to check your pulse and make sure you stay within your target heart rate zone.

Target Heart Rate Zone: Typically, 50-85% of your maximum heart rate (MHR). To calculate your MHR, subtract your age from 220.

2. Progress Tracking

Keep a detailed log of your cardio workouts to monitor your progress. Track the duration, intensity (e.g., speed or resistance level), and perceived exertion for each session to see how your endurance and fitness levels improve over time.

Tools: Apps like Strava or fitness journals can help with regular tracking and comparison.

4. Incorporating Rest Days

Rest is just as important as your workouts. Ensure you allow for proper recovery between intense cardio sessions. This helps prevent injury and promotes long-term improvements in endurance and performance.

Recommended: Take 1-2 days of rest each week, especially after high-intensity sessions.

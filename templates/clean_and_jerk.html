The Clean and Jerk

Understanding the Clean and Jerk

The Clean and Jerk is a two-part Olympic lift where the barbell is first lifted to the shoulders (Clean) and then pressed overhead (Jerk). This movement tests strength, technique, and power.

Benefits: Builds total body strength, explosiveness, and coordination, while improving flexibility and balance.

Step-by-Step Technique
Clean:
  • Setup: Stand with feet shoulder-width apart, grip the barbell slightly wider than shoulder-width.
  • First Pull: Lift the barbell off the ground by extending your knees and hips.
  • Transition: Pull the barbell towards your body as it passes your knees.
  • Second Pull: Explosively extend your hips and legs while pulling the barbell to your shoulders.
  • Catch: Drop into a squat, catching the barbell on your shoulders. Stand up to finish the Clean.
Jerk:
  • Setup: With the bar resting on your shoulders, dip your knees slightly to prepare for the overhead press.
  • Drive: Explosively drive through your legs, pressing the barbell overhead.
  • Catch: Drop into a lunge or squat position under the bar to stabilize.
  • Finish: Lock out your arms overhead and bring your feet back together to complete the lift.
Common Mistakes to Avoid
  • Poor bar path: Keep the barbell close to your body to maintain control and balance.
  • Insufficient leg drive: Use your legs to generate power, especially in the jerk portion.

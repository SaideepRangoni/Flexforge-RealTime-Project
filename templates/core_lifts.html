Core Lifts in Powerlifting

Squat

Description: The squat is a fundamental lower-body exercise in powerlifting. It involves lowering your body by bending at the knees and hips, then returning to a standing position.

Technique: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart. Keep your chest up and back straight. Lower yourself by bending at the knees and hips until your thighs are parallel to the ground, then push through your heels to return to the starting position.

Benefits: Builds strength in the legs, glutes, and core. Enhances overall lower body power and stability.

Bench Press

Description: The bench press is a key upper-body exercise where you press a barbell away from your chest while lying on a bench.

Technique: Lie on a bench with your feet flat on the ground. Grip the barbell slightly wider than shoulder-width. Lower the barbell to your chest, then press it back up to arm's length while keeping your back flat against the bench.

Benefits: Strengthens the chest, shoulders, and triceps. Improves pushing power and upper body stability.

Deadlift

Description: The deadlift is a full-body exercise where you lift a barbell from the ground to hip level, primarily targeting the back, legs, and core.

Technique: Stand with your feet hip-width apart. Grip the barbell with your hands just outside your knees. Keeping your back straight, lift the barbell by extending your hips and knees until you are standing upright. Lower the barbell back to the ground with control.

Benefits: Builds strength in the back, legs, and core. Enhances overall body power and functional strength.

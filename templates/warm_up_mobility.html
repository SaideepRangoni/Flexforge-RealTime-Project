Warm-Up and Mobility for Olympic Weightlifting

Warm-Up Overview

Before starting any weightlifting session, itâ€™s crucial to perform a thorough warm-up. Warming up helps increase blood flow to the muscles, improve flexibility, and prepare your joints for the heavy loads of Olympic weightlifting.

Steps to a Proper Warm-Up:

  • General Warm-Up: Start with 5-10 minutes of light cardio (jogging, cycling, or rowing) to get your heart rate up and blood flowing to the muscles.
  • Dynamic Stretching: Perform dynamic stretches that target the muscle groups you'll be using. This can include arm swings, leg swings, shoulder circles, and hip rotations.
  • Mobility Drills: Focus on improving the range of motion in critical areas like the shoulders, hips, and ankles. Exercises such as overhead squats with a PVC pipe, hip flexor stretches, and ankle mobility drills are essential.
Mobility for crossfit

Mobility is key to performing Olympic lifts safely and effectively. Adequate mobility in the shoulders, hips, and ankles allows for better form and deeper positions in both the snatch and clean & jerk.

Key Mobility Areas:

  • Shoulders: Improve shoulder mobility with stretches like shoulder dislocations using a resistance band or PVC pipe.
  • Hips: Hip flexibility is crucial for achieving deep squat positions. Use hip openers like the pigeon pose or deep lunges to enhance hip mobility.
  • Ankles: Ankle mobility affects your squat depth and balance. Perform ankle dorsiflexion exercises or use a resistance band to improve mobility in this area.

Include these mobility drills in your warm-up routine to ensure you're primed for the technical demands of Olympic weightlifting.
